Orang Utan

Where it can be found: Tropical rainforests in Borneo (Indonesia, Malaysia) and Sumatra (Indonesia). Number left in the wild: Bornean Orangutan: Around 104,700 individuals. Sumatran Orangutan: Only about 14,000 individuals. Tapanuli Orangutan: Fewer than 800 individuals. Reasons it's endangered: Deforestation due to palm oil plantations and illegal logging. Hunting and illegal pet trade. Habitat destruction due to forest fires. What we can do: Support sustainable palm oil products. Donate to conservation programs. Raise awareness about protecting their habitats.

Tapir

Where it can be found: Tropical forests in Malaysia, Thailand, Myanmar, and Sumatra (Indonesia). Number left in the wild: Fewer than 2,500 individuals. Reasons it's endangered: Habitat loss due to deforestation and agriculture. Road accidents. Poaching, although it is less common. What we can do: Protect and expand forest reserves. Build safe corridors for wildlife to prevent road accidents. Educate people about the importance of tapirs in ecosystems.

Malayan Tiger

Where it can be found: Forests of Peninsular Malaysia. Number left in the wild: Fewer than 150 individuals. Reasons it's endangered: Poaching for fur, bones, and traditional medicine. Habitat destruction due to agriculture and logging. Loss of prey animals. What we can do: Strengthen anti-poaching laws. Support conservation breeding programs. Protect and restore tiger habitats.

Rhinoceros Hornbill

Where it can be found: Rainforests of Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, and Borneo. Number left in the wild: Exact population unknown, but declining rapidly. Reasons it's endangered: Habitat destruction due to logging and agriculture. Hunting for its beak, which is used in decorations and traditional beliefs. What we can do: Ban illegal wildlife trade. Preserve rainforest habitats. Educate local communities about the importance of hornbills.

Sun Bear

Where it can be found: Forests of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia, and Myanmar. Number left in the wild: Estimated 2,500 - 10,000 individuals. Reasons it's endangered: Habitat destruction due to deforestation. Poaching for bile, which is used in traditional medicine. Illegal pet trade. What we can do: Support anti-poaching efforts. Raise awareness about the cruelty of bear bile farming. Protect forest habitats.
